## AusSora Assessment Process

### 0. Pre-application Meeting

### 1. CONOPs Development

operational area + contingency volume = operational volume + ground risk buffer

### 2. Ground Risk Class (GRC) Assessment

- **iGRC** - initial (risks before safety measures)
- **fGRC** - final (risks after safety measures) - population density, cross reference qualitative descriptor, iGRC table,
  - sheltering M1 / impact reduction M2

### 3. Air Risk Class (ARC) Assessment

- determine Airspace Encounter Category (AEC) (depends on airspace type and likelihood of encounters)
- 🔗 [JARUS SORA v2.5 Main Body (PDF)](http://jarus-rpas.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/SORA-v2.5-Main-Body-Release-JAR_doc_25.pdf)
- aim: by reducing likelihood on encounter - flight restriction based on stakeholder engagement / NVLOS / deconfliction plans

### 4. Specific Assurance and Integrity Level (SAIL) Determination

Level 1–6

- SAIL rating reflects potential risks, higher rating = stricter safety requirement of drone + training + risk management

### 5. Operational Safety Objectives (OSOs)

(manage risks related to out of control situations)

- **integrity**: quality of the safety objectives
- **assurance**: level of confidence in achieving the objectives
- **robustness**: level of robustness combines integrity and assurance (optional, low, medium, high)

### 6. Containment Assessment

(manage risks within adjacent area if OSOs fail)

- operation is expected to end if a loss of control occurs beyond the ground risk buffer
- includes requirements based on the population at risk within the adjacent area.

### 7. Emergency Response Plan (ERP)

(controls consequences once the drone is out of control)

- details how local authorities, such as fire brigade and ATC, will respond.
- limit the residual effects and harm from an out-of-control event.

---

## Demonstrate Compliance

- submit AusSORA based safety case, including application form and checklist
- give evidence supporting mitigations for GRC and ARC
- give evidence/declaration for OSOs at the determined SAIL
- meet the containment requirement for the ops area

---

## Population Density Determination

(highest population density for a specific area)

- use the latest Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) Census data - they provide smallest grid size of 1 km by 1 km
- if near populated areas, it requires (higher mitigation method - sheltering/exclusion zones, more robust contingency plans, consider dynamic populations - crowds and roadways)

---

## Drone Characteristics and Dimensions for GRC

Use:

- wingspan (aeroplane/power-lift)
- blade diameter (single rotor heli)
- max distance between blade tips (multi-rotor heli)
- ensure max speed = highest possible commanded airspeed
  - 🔗 [RPAS Compliance](https://www.casa.gov.au/drones/flight-authorisations/remotely-piloted-aircraft-systems-compliance)

## BVLOS Training Areas

(low-risk location as large as necessary - train maintain competency and currency for all staff)

- encourage seek approval of an area for the purpose of training / testing.
- generally issue these approvals for the validity of your ReOC.
