# Pre-Flight: NAIPS Account Setup and NOTAMs

Once your CASA approval is in hand, your next step before each operation is checking airspace conditions and submitting NOTAMs where required. Both of these happen through NAIPS — the system run by Airservices Australia that provides real-time aeronautical data, weather, and NOTAMs.

This guide covers setting up your NAIPS account, running pre-flight briefings, and submitting NOTAMs for your drone operations.

Author: Rachel Ng

Last review: 3 May 2026

---

## What Is NAIPS?

NAIPS (National Aeronautical Information Processing System) is the central platform for Australian aviation information. As a ReOC holder, you'll use it to:

- Check NOTAMs affecting your operational area
- Access weather data (TAFs, METARs)
- Verify your own NOTAM is active before flying
- Access aviation charts and briefing products

Registration is free and you should set this up well before your first operation — don't leave it to the day before a job.

---

## Creating Your NAIPS Account

1. Go to the [NAIPS registration page](https://www.airservicesaustralia.com/naips/Account/Register)
2. Register a username and password and provide your contact details
3. Once confirmed, log in at the [NAIPS Internet Service (NIS)](https://www.airservicesaustralia.com/naips/Account/Logon)
4. Keep your credentials secure — you're responsible for any activity under your account

> If you need help with NAIPS at any point, contact the Airservices Service Desk (24/7) on **1800 801 960**.

## Pre-Flight Briefings

Before every operation, log into NAIPS and run a briefing for your area. There are two types:

**Location Briefing** — Provides information relating to a specific aerodrome and its immediate vicinity (5 NM radius). Use this when operating near a known aerodrome.

**Area Briefing** — Covers a broader region. Use this when your operation isn't near a specific aerodrome, or when you want a wider picture of activity in the area.

### What to Check

- **NOTAMs** — Any temporary or permanent changes to airspace that affect your site (restricted areas, military exercises, emergency operations, other drone operations, etc.)
- **Weather** — TAFs (Terminal Area Forecasts) and METARs for the nearest reporting station. Cross-reference with BOM and your own on-site assessment.
- **Other activity** — Look for NOTAMs from other operators in your area that might create conflicting traffic.

> **Important:** Not all aerodromes have a NOTAM service. If you request a briefing for an aerodrome without one, NAIPS will return "A NOTAM service is not provided for the following location." This doesn't mean there are no NOTAMs — it means that aerodrome isn't covered by the service.

## Submitting NOTAMs for Drone Operations

Many CASA area approvals include a condition requiring you to publish a NOTAM before each operation. Check your approval instrument — it will specify whether a NOTAM is required and what information to include.

### Timing

Submit your NOTAM at least **2 business days** before the flight. Allow time for any back-and-forth with the NOTAM office. CASA will provide specific guidance on the submission process in their approval email.

### How to Submit

There are two methods:

**NOTAM Request Form (PDF)** — Download the form from the [Airservices NOTAM Originator page](https://www.airservicesaustralia.com/industry-info/flight-briefing/notam-originator/) and submit as directed by CASA. The current version (V14, published February 2025) is available on that page.

**NOTAM Web Service (NWS)** — Airservices has rolled out an online submission system within NAIPS. Authorised originators can enter NOTAM information directly via a web form instead of emailing PDFs. This is faster and reduces the risk of errors. NWS is currently used by RPAS operators, CASA, Airservices, the ADF, and about 60% of certified aerodromes. Check with CASA whether you're set up to use NWS for your operations.

### On the Day

Before you fly:

- [ ] Log into NAIPS and confirm your NOTAM is active and the details are correct
- [ ] Check for any new NOTAMs in your area published since your last briefing
- [ ] Check weather (TAF/METAR) and confirm conditions are within your operating limits
- [ ] If your NOTAM isn't showing or details are wrong, contact the Airservices Service Desk on 1800 801 960 before proceeding

## NAIPS on Mobile

The **NAIPS app** (iOS, developed by OzRunways) lets you access briefings, submit flight notifications, and check NOTAMs from your phone. Useful for on-site checks on the day. You still need your NAIPS login credentials.

Other CASA-verified apps like **OzRunways** and **AvPlan** also integrate NOTAM data from Airservices Australia, so you may already have access through your existing planning tools.
