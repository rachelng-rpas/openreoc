# References — CASA Source Documents

This page lists the primary CASA regulatory documents referenced throughout this guide. Always check the [Federal Register of Legislation](https://www.legislation.gov.au/) and the [CASA website](https://www.casa.gov.au/) for the most current versions.

---

## Primary Legislation and Standards

### CASR Part 101 — Unmanned Aircraft and Rockets

The Civil Aviation Safety Regulations 1998, Part 101 is the primary regulatory instrument governing RPA operations in Australia, including certified (ReOC) operations.

Key regulations referenced in this guide:

| Regulation | Topic |
|---|---|
| 101.295 | ReOC holder must comply with MOS 101 |
| 101.335 | Requirement to maintain an operations manual |
| 101.340 | Chief Remote Pilot — appointment and responsibilities |
| 101.342 | Personnel requirements, including records of qualifications |
| 101.345 | Obligation to keep records in accordance with MOS 101 |

**Where to find it:** [Federal Register of Legislation — CASR Part 101](https://www.legislation.gov.au/Series/F1998B00220)

### MOS 101 — Manual of Standards Part 101

The Manual of Standards Part 101 provides detailed operational standards that supplement CASR Part 101. **Chapter 10** is the primary source for record-keeping requirements.

Key sections referenced in this guide:

| Section | Topic |
|---|---|
| s10.01 | Purpose and scope of Chapter 10 |
| s10.02 | Definitions and interpretation |
| s10.03 | General record-keeping requirements |
| s10.03(1) | Operational documents (JSA, RA, flight plan) |
| s10.03(2) | Personnel and training records |
| s10.04 | RPAS operational release (pre-flight authorisation) |
| s10.04(2) | Release approval requirement |
| s10.04(5) | Sole RP exception for release approval |
| s10.04(6) | Sub-2kg VLOS exception |
| s10.05 | RPAS operational log (post-flight record) |
| s10.05(2) | Sub-2kg VLOS exception for operational log |
| s10.06 | Remote pilot log (individual pilot record) |
| s10.07 | RPAS technical log (aircraft maintenance and serviceability) |
| s10.08 | Training of non-RP crew (observers, ground handlers, loading staff) |

**Where to find it:** [CASA — Manual of Standards Part 101](https://www.casa.gov.au/search-centre/manuals-of-standards)

## Advisory Material

### AC 101-01 — Unmanned Aircraft and Rockets

Advisory Circular 101-01 provides guidance on complying with CASR Part 101. While not legally binding, it represents CASA's recommended approach and is widely used as an interpretive reference.

**Current version at time of writing:** v6.0

**Where to find it:** [CASA — Advisory Circulars](https://www.casa.gov.au/search-centre/advisory-circulars)

## Other Instruments Referenced

### CASA RPAS Sample Operations Manual

CASA provides a sample operations manual that includes template record-keeping procedures. This can be a useful starting point for developing your own operations manual and understanding what CASA expects in terms of record formats and procedures.

**Where to find it:** Available on the CASA website under RPAS resources.

### EX45/24 (as amended by EX92/25)

A CASA exemption instrument relevant to certain RPA operations. If your operations rely on this instrument, keep a copy on file and reference it in your operational records where applicable.

**Expiry:** 31 July 2027 (at time of writing — verify current status)

### TMI 2024-01

A CASA Temporary Management Instruction relevant to RPAS operations. Check the CASA website for current status and applicability.

## How to Stay Current

Regulations change. To stay informed:

1. **Subscribe to CASA's email updates** — CASA sends notifications about regulatory changes, new advisory circulars, and exemption instruments.

2. **Check the Federal Register of Legislation** periodically — this is the authoritative source for current regulations and standards.

3. **Review CASA's RPAS page** — [casa.gov.au/drones](https://www.casa.gov.au/drones) — for news, guidance, and updates specific to drone operations.

4. **Check this repository** — we aim to update references when significant regulatory changes occur, but always verify against official sources.
