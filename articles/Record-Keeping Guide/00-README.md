# OpenReOC — Record-Keeping Guide for Australian RPA Operators

**A free, plain-English guide to CASA-compliant record keeping under CASR Part 101 and MOS 101 (Chapter 10).**

Last reviewed: 17 April 2026

---

## Who Is This For?

This guide is written for **ReOC holders**, particularly small operators or sole operators who need to maintain proper records but may not use (or be ready to pay for) commercial compliance tools like AVCRM, AirData, or similar platforms.

If you're managing records with spreadsheets, paper logs, or a simple folder on Google Drive, this guide is for you.

## What Does It Cover?

This documentation package explains:

1. **What CASA actually requires**: in plain English, referenced back to MOS 101 Chapter 10 and CASR Part 101.
2. **What types of records you need to keep**: operational records, training records, technical logs, pilot logs, and more.
3. **How to set up a practical system**:  folder structures, naming conventions, and workflows for both paper-based and simple digital systems.
4. **How to use the provided templates**: field-by-field guidance mapped to CASA requirements.
5. **How to stay compliant** — internal audits, review cycles, and common mistakes to avoid.
6. **Best practice beyond the minimums**: what good operators do, even when CASA doesn't explicitly require it.

## Key CASA References

This guide draws primarily from:

- **CASR Part 101**: Civil Aviation Safety Regulations, Subpart 101.F (Certified RPA operators)
- **MOS 101 Chapter 10**: Manual of Standards for Part 101, Chapter 10: Records and documents
- **AC 101-01 v6.0**: Advisory Circular for unmanned aircraft and rockets
- **Regulation 101.342(b)**: Personnel record requirements for ReOC holders

All section references (e.g. "s10.04") refer to MOS 101 Chapter 10 unless stated otherwise.
