# References — CASA Source Documents

This page lists the primary CASA regulatory documents referenced throughout this guide. Always check the Federal Register of Legislation and the CASA website for the most current versions.

---

## Primary Legislation and Standards

### CASR Part 101

The CASR Part 101 is the regulatory instrument governing RPA and certified ReOC operations.

Key regulations referenced in this guide:

| Regulation | Topic |
|---|---|
| 101.295 | ReOC holder must comply with MOS 101 |
| 101.335 | Requirement to maintain an operations manual |
| 101.340 | Chief Remote Pilot — appointment and responsibilities |
| 101.342 | Personnel requirements, including records of qualifications |
| 101.345 | Obligation to keep records in accordance with MOS 101 |

**Where to find it:** [Federal Register of Legislation — CASR Part 101](https://www.legislation.gov.au/Series/F1998B00220)

### MOS 101

The MOS101 provides detailed operational standards that supplement CASR Part 101. **Chapter 10** is the primary source for record-keeping requirements.

Key sections referenced in this guide:

| Section | Topic |
|---|---|
| s10.03 | General record-keeping requirements |
| s10.03(1) | Operational documents (JSA, RA, flight plan) |
| s10.03(2) | Personnel and internal training records |
| s10.04 | RPAS operational release (pre-flight authorisation) |
| s10.04(2) | Release approval requirement |
| s10.04(5) | Sole RP exception for release approval |
| s10.04(6) | Sub-2kg VLOS exception |
| s10.05 | RPAS operational log (post-flight record) |
| s10.05(2) | Sub-2kg VLOS exception for operational log |
| s10.06 | Remote pilot log (individual pilot record) |
| s10.07 | RPAS technical log (aircraft maintenance and serviceability) |
| s10.08 | Training of non-RP crew (observers, ground handlers, loading staff) |

**Where to find it:** [[CASA — Manual of Standards Part 101](https://www.legislation.gov.au/F2019L00593/latest/text)]

## Advisory Material

### AC 101-01 — Unmanned Aircraft and Rockets

Advisory Circular 101-01 provides guidance on compliance. While not legally binding, it represents CASA's recommended approach and is widely used as an interpretive reference.

**Current version at time of writing:** v6.0

**Where to find it:** [CASA — Advisory Circulars](https://www.casa.gov.au/search-centre/advisory-circulars)

## How to Stay Current

Regulations change. To stay informed:

1. **Subscribe to CASA's email updates** — CASA sends notifications about regulatory changes, new advisory circulars, and exemption instruments.

2. **Check the Federal Register of Legislation** periodically — this is the authoritative source for current regulations and standards.

3. **Review CASA's RPAS page** — [casa.gov.au/drones](https://www.casa.gov.au/drones) — for news, guidance, and updates specific to drone operations.
