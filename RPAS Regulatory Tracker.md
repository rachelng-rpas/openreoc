| 1 | Apr 2026 | Release | AC 101-06v1 – AusSORA Assessment Requirements and Criteria | [CASA](https://www.casa.gov.au/drones/flight-authorisations/about-our-assessment-framework) | Outlines the AusSORA framework adopted from JARUS SORA 2.5. | 3 | May 2024 | Policy | JARUS guidelines on Specific Operations Risk Assessment  (SORA) | [JARUS](chrome-extension://efaidnbmnnnibpcajpcglclefindmkaj/http://jarus-rpas.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/SORA-v2.5-Main-Body-Release-JAR_doc_25.pdf) | The core risk assessment methodology for Category B (Specific) UAS operations. It walks through how to evaluate a concept of operation and categorise it into one of 6 SAIL levels. The process covers defining operational volumes and ground risk buffers, assessing ground risk (iGRC → fGRC with mitigations), assessing air risk (ARC via airspace encounter categories), determining the SAIL, then setting operational safety objectives (OSOs) with required robustness levels. Version 2.5 restructured containment into its own step, removed ERP as a mitigation, updated the iGRC table based on quantitative methods from Annex F, and incorporated cyber (Annex E) and UTM concepts (Annex H). This is the document CASA's AusSORA framework is adopted from. |
| 4 | June 2019 | Policy | JARUS UAS Operational Categorization | [JARUS](chrome-extension://efaidnbmnnnibpcajpcglclefindmkaj/http://jarus-rpas.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/jar_10_doc_UAS_Operational_Cat.pdf)| The foundational document that establishes the three-tier categorisation scheme — Open (A), Specific (B), Certified (C) — based on unmitigated risk level. It defines what level of regulatory involvement applies at each tier: from self-certification with operational limitations in Open, through authority-assessed risk-based approvals in Specific, to full type certification and airworthiness like manned aviation in Certified. It also covers the risk mitigation strategies available across categories — airworthiness, operational limitations, operator competence, identification, and design approvals. This is the "why" document behind the categories; the SORA is the "how" for Category B specifically. |
